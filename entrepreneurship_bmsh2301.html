What is Entrepreneurship?

From the French "entreprendre" ‚Äî "to undertake." Modern definition: creating, organizing, and operating a new business venture to generate profit while taking on financial risk.

Entrepreneurs introduce new goods, services, or concepts to the market to make a profit, taking risks to achieve their goals.

Types of Innovation

1. Process Innovation ‚ñæ
Change in how a product/service is made or distributed for greater efficiency.
Ex: Self-checkout, online booking, robotic automation
2. Product/Service Innovation ‚ñæ
New ideas to enhance customer value & differentiate market offerings.
Ex: iPhone (2007), Plant-based meat, Netflix streaming, Grab
3. Disruptive Innovation ‚ñæ
Low-end: Affordable alternatives (Budget airlines, Xiaomi phones)
New-market: Restricted product unlocked (GoPro, WhatsApp)

Product Costing

Three components:

  • Direct Materials ‚Äî Raw materials (wood, plastic, fabric)
  • Direct Labor ‚Äî Wages of production workers
  • Overhead Costs ‚Äî Indirect materials, indirect labor, other factory costs
Product Cost = Direct Materials + Direct Labor + Overhead
Cost per Unit = Total Product Cost √∑ Units Produced
üè®

Service Costing

  • Fixed Costs ‚Äî Rent, salaries, insurance (don't change)
  • Semi-variable ‚Äî Mix of fixed and variable (labor)
  • Variable Costs ‚Äî Change with production volume
Total Service Cost = Fixed + Semi-Variable + Variable
Cost/Unit = Total Cost √∑ Total Service Units

Units: Hotels=Room Days, Transport=Passenger-km, Hospital=Per Patient

  • Premium ‚Äî High price = perception of luxury (Gucci)
  • Economy ‚Äî Low price, target budget buyers (Puregold)
  • Price Skimming ‚Äî Start high, reduce over time (Apple iPhone)
  • Penetration ‚Äî Start low, raise later (Netflix)
  • Psychological ‚Äî ‚Ç±99 instead of ‚Ç±100
  • Bundle ‚Äî Multiple products at discount
  • Dynamic ‚Äî Adjusts to demand (Grab, Angkas)
